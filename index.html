About

I am Mohammad Rakibur Rahman, a Master's student in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Oulu. My research focuses on computer vision, thermal imaging, and multimodal sensing for non-contact physiological monitoring.

I work on developing real-time sensing systems that combine thermal cameras, RGB-D sensors, and biosensors to study physiological dynamics and autonomic activity.

Master's Thesis

Development of a Multimodal System for Non-Contact Physiological Monitoring

This research investigates whether thermoregulation-driven temperature dynamics from facial and palm regions can be used as non-contact indicators of autonomic nervous system activity.

The system integrates thermal imaging, RGB-D cameras, and wearable biosensors to analyze relationships between skin temperature dynamics, respiration, and electrodermal activity.
